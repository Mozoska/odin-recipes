Classic Dauphinoise Potatoes: A Creamy, Cheesy Delight

Here's our recipe for Dauphinoise Potatoes, a beloved French classic that promises to be the star of any meal. Thinly sliced potatoes are layered with garlic, fresh thyme, and generously bathed in a rich, creamy sauce, then baked until tender and golden brown. The edges become wonderfully crisp while the inside remains luxuriously soft and infused with flavor. This dish is the ultimate comforting side, perfect for a cozy family dinner or an elegant gathering. Simple to assemble and utterly satisfying, Dauphinoise Potatoes are sure to impress.

Classic Dauphinoise Potatoes Recipe

Prep Time: 20-25 minutes

Cook Time: 45-60 minutes

Total Time: Approximately 1 hour 5 minutes - 1 hour 25 minutes

Servings: 6-8 people

Ingredients

  • 1.5 kg (about 3.3 lbs) starchy potatoes (like Russet or Yukon Gold), peeled and thinly sliced (about 2-3 mm thick)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced or finely grated
  • 500 ml (about 2 cups) heavy cream (35% fat)
  • 250 ml (about 1 cup) whole milk
  • 100 g (about 1 cup) Gruyère cheese, grated (or Comté, or a good quality cheddar)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves, picked (or 1 tsp dried thyme)
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, for greasing the dish

Instructions

1. Prepare the Potatoes and Cream Mixture:

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Grease a large gratin dish (approximately 25x35 cm or 10x14 inches) generously with butter.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the **heavy cream**, **whole milk**, **minced garlic**, **thyme leaves**, **nutmeg**, **salt**, and **pepper**. Whisk well to combine. Taste the mixture and adjust seasoning as needed – remember the potatoes will absorb a lot of the flavor.
  4. Add the **thinly sliced potatoes** to the cream mixture and toss gently to ensure all potato slices are coated.

2. Assemble the Dauphinoise:

  1. Arrange about **half of the potato slices** in a single, even layer in the prepared gratin dish. Try to overlap them slightly for a nice presentation.
  2. Sprinkle about **half of the grated Gruyère cheese** evenly over the potatoes.
  3. Carefully pour about **half of the remaining cream mixture** over this layer.
  4. Repeat with the **remaining potato slices**, arranging them in a second layer.
  5. Pour the **rest of the cream mixture** evenly over the second layer of potatoes.
  6. Sprinkle the **remaining Gruyère cheese** over the top.

3. Bake the Dauphinoise Potatoes:

  1. Place the gratin dish on a baking sheet (to catch any potential overflow).
  2. Bake for 45 to 60 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork, and the top is beautifully golden brown and bubbly. If the top browns too quickly, you can loosely cover it with aluminum foil for the last 15-20 minutes of baking.
  3. Once cooked, remove from the oven and let it **rest for 10-15 minutes** before serving. This allows the creamy sauce to set and makes for easier serving.

Chef's Tips:

  • Slicing is key: For best results, use a mandoline to get perfectly even, thin slices of potato. This ensures even cooking.
  • Don't rinse the potatoes: Unlike some potato dishes, do not rinse the sliced potatoes. The starch helps to thicken the sauce naturally.
  • Cheese variations: While Gruyère is traditional, you can also use Comté, Emmental, or even a good quality sharp cheddar for a different flavor profile. Some recipes even skip the cheese entirely in the layers, only adding it on top or not at all, to keep it truly "gratin dauphinois" (without cheese).
  • Garlic infusion: For a more subtle garlic flavor, you can rub the inside of the baking dish with a cut garlic clove before buttering.
  • Resting time: Don't skip the resting time! It's crucial for the sauce to set and prevents the potatoes from falling apart when served.
